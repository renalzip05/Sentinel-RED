The Sun. Is it a problem?

“Solar conjunction”, when the Sun blocks any information sent from Earth to Mars, or the other way around. Data sent between this period will be lost, including commands sent to robots, causing confusion in these, making them execute tasks incorrectly. Luckily, it is not permanent and only lasts a few weeks.

(NASA Science, 2020)

NASA'S Deep Space Network.

Conformed by 3 facilities that connect Earth to spacecrafts. They are key for communicating to Mars. It is used to control spacecraft remotely, also to send and receive data. Without it, many interplanetary missions could not be possible.

(NASA, 2020)

Transmitting to the Moon…

Communications to the Moon are like those that go all the way to Mars. Instead, they are supported by the Near Earth Network (NEN), which has the same ground system concept of antennas placed in key spots, like the Deep Space Network (DSN).

(NASA, 2018)

Side-to-site communication.

Communication to Mars right now is done from side to side (NASA 2020).
What benefits does it present?

  • No risks in losing singla retransmission spacecraft.
  • The money saved on retransmission craft can be invested in other projects.
What are the disadvantages of the current method?
  • Data may be los along the way (like when solar conjunctions happen).
  • Information's travel is slower than if we had retransmission points.

Internet on Mars.

Interplanetary internet usage may no longer be a distant thought as optical communication systems (10-100 times faster than current radio frequency) are being developed. Sending e-mails, text messages, and others from one planet to another will no longer be a fantasy.

(NASA, 2020)
